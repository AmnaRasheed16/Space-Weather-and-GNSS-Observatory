Geomagnetic storms disrupt Earth's magnetic envelope, causing massive currents in the upper atmosphere. These perturbations trigger local ionospheric scintillation, causing signal dispersion and tracking loss in receivers. Single-frequency GPS devices suffer severe ranging anomalies under storm conditions.

Diurnal ionospheric fluctuations and absolute electron concentration anomalies.

24-Hour Regional TEC Trends (TECU)

Understanding TEC Variations:

1 TECU represents 1016 electrons/m² along the signal path. Diurnal peaks occur during local solar noon due to ultraviolet photoionization. Sudden Ionospheric Disturbances (SIDs) trigger sharp increases, inducing range errors up to several meters in single-frequency GNSS receivers.
